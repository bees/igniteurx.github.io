Speakers

Jose Cong
José Benitez Cong is an HR veteran with more than 15 years of experience recruiting the teams behind some of the most recognized consumer electronics products in history. He spent six years at Apple, organizing the growth efforts behind the launch of the iPod, iPhone, and iOS cameras. In 2011, José joined Nest Labs to manage talent during the launch of the critically acclaimed Nest Learning Thermostat and Nest Protect. While at Nest, he developed Nestling, a highly sought after internship program. He transitioned the team through the acquisition by Google for $3.2B in early 2014. Currently, José is heading up Plause, a startup he co-founded to change the way employees and companies approach employee feedback and performance reviews.

Former Head of Talent Nest
Plause

Farouk Dey
Dr. Farouk Dey is Associate Vice Provost of Student Affairs and Dean of Career Education at Stanford University. He is leading the expansion of career education at Stanford and implementing a new vision and a reinvented model of career connections and meaningful work for college students and alumni. Dr. Dey has held executive leadership roles at Stanford, Carnegie Mellon, the University of Florida, the National Association for Colleges and Employers (NACE), and the American College Personnel Association (ACPA). His credentials include a PhD in Higher Education Administration, EdS in Higher Education Administration, MBA, MEd in Counseling Psychology, and BBA in Finance. Author of several articles and book chapters, Dr. Dey has served as a consultant at many organizations and universities, faculty at several institutes, and keynote speaker at many conferences in the US and around the world.

AVP & Dean of Career Education
Stanford University

Beth Scheer
Beth is the Head of Talent at Homebrew a seed stage venture firm with a focus on the "Bottom Up Economy". Prior to Homebrew, Beth spent 5+ years at Salesforce leading executive search, sales leadership, and sales growth/professional services recruiting teams. She joined Google in 2003 to build out the AdSense team and then spent 6 years hiring for business operations, corporate communications, corporate development, and various engineering teams, including new graduate PhDs. She is a a proud graduate of The Colorado College with a BS in Psychology.

Head of Talent
Homebrew

Joslin Torrano
Josilin Torrano has been an in-house Recruiter and University Program Manager for eight years. Beginning her career as a University Relations intern while a student at UCLA. Since 2008, she has partnered with the best and brightest minds in the entertainment and tech industries to find exceptional talent. What she loves most about her job is connecting with people and helping them along in their careers. At Facebook, Joslin travels the world seeking top design, content strategy, and marketing talent. Having worked with professors, administrators and recrutiing partners, she understands the value of strong university relationships. Joslin has been featured in Vault, Career Contessa, Refinery29, Thought Café, Make It New: A History of Silicon Valley Design, and the Miracle Worker series.

University Product Recruiting Lead
Facebook

Tey Scott
Tey currently leads the Strategic Global Talent Acquisition Programs team at Linkedin, the company that has helped 450 million professionals in the pursuit of creating economic prosperity and being more productive at work. Strategic Global Programs consists of 5 marquee teams built on programs that manifest hires for LinkedIn’s overall hiring goals; Campus Recruiting, Diversity Recruiting, Employee Referral Recruiting along with an events and recruiting operations team that supports the whole TA engine. Prior to LinkedIn, led North American Staffing teams for Citrix Systems & AOL.

Director Talent Acquisition
Linkedin

Jeff D'Andria
Jeff connects Stanford students to opportunities by creating inroads for Tech and Startup employers on campus. Before his time at Stanford, he was a technical recruiter at Microsoft for universities across Canada and the Pacific Northwest.

Career Ventures Tech & Startups
Stanford Career Eductation

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the conference about?

URx is is a conference bringing together university recruiting professionals in the tech industry.

What the heck does "URx" stand for?

UR = University Recruiting and x is our theme for the conference. This year x = Tech Recruiting.

How Can I Volunteer at URx?

We are always happy to have passionate people get involved and share their talents. You can find the volunteer application here.

When and Where is URx?

URx is May 2-3, 2017 at Lyft HQ in San Francisco. Lyft is located at 185 Berry Street.

How do I get to Lyft Headquarters?

Lyft HQ is conveniently located in the SoMa neighborhood in the city of San Francisco. Lyft is a short walk from the Caltrain Station and Light Rail at 4th & King Street. Parking is limited, so we advise against driving and suggest you take a Lyft 😉 .

Do you offer refunds?

We offer full refunds for your ticket before March 1st. We will not be able to provide refunds after the 1st of March. You are always welcome to transfer your ticket to someone else, simply let us know and we will process the request.

What meals are provided?

Light breakfast, lunch, snacks, and all the coffee you can drink throughout the conference are included with your registration. Please let us know if you have any special dietary requirements. We are happy to accommodate any dietary requests.

Who should attend?

Unversity recruiters, University administrators (involved in employer relations, employer development, etc), Student Organization Leaders, and organizations that help promote or facillitate connections between students and employers.

Who organizes URx?

URx is organized by a group of university administrators and university recruiters with support from great partners, volunteers, and sponsors.

How Can I Speak at URx?

We are always happy to have passionate people get involved and share their expertise! If you are interested in speaking, you can find the speaker application here.

How Can I Become a Sponsor or Host a Trek?

We are excited to consider opportunities that will enhance the conference experience. For sponosorship, please fill out the sponsor interest form. If you are interested in hosting a trek on site at your company please fill out the trek interest form.

Will you be selling tickets at the door?

Unfortunately we won't. To achieve our goal of a true intimate conference experiecne we are limiting attendance to 250 people. We anticipate tickets will sell out. It is recommended that you buy your tickets early to guarantee your spot.
