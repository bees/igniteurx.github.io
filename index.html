Speakers

Our wonderful speakers and keynotes are a diverse group of student,university, and employer talent leaders.

Jose Cong
José Benitez Cong is an HR veteran with more than 15 years of experience recruiting the teams behind some of the most recognized consumer electronics products in history. He spent six years at Apple, organizing the growth efforts behind the launch of the iPod, iPhone, and iOS cameras. In 2011, José joined Nest Labs to manage talent during the launch of the critically acclaimed Nest Learning Thermostat and Nest Protect. While at Nest, he developed Nestling, a highly sought after internship program. He transitioned the team through the acquisition by Google for $3.2B in early 2014. Currently, José is heading up Plause, a startup he co-founded to change the way employees and companies approach employee feedback and performance reviews.

Co-Founder, Plause. Former Head of Talent Nest (Acq. Google) & University Recruiting Lead Apple

Jeff D'Andria
Jeff connects Stanford students to opportunities by creating inroads for Tech and Startup employers on campus. Before his time at Stanford, he was a technical recruiter at Microsoft for universities across Canada and the Pacific Northwest.

Career Ventures BEAM, Stanford Career Eductation. Former University Recruiter Microsoft  

Kate Randall
Kate is an accomplished university recruiting professional. Prior to joining Microsoft as a Senior University Recruiting Manger, she was at Tesla motors. At Tesla, Kate built the university programs recruitment function. Kate began her career in university recruiting as a talent coordinator at Electronic Arts.

Senior University Recruiting Manager, Microsoft    Microsoft University Recruiting

Andrew Pryor-Miller

Quality Assurance Engineer, Snapchat

Phillip Loya
Phillip joined CODE2040 from UC Berkeley’s Incentive Awards Program, the largest privately funded recruitment, retention, scholarship, and degree completion program in the UC System. He conducted outreach and recruitment to primarily low-income, first-generation, underrepresented students at various high schools throughout California. Originally from the Boyle Heights neighborhood in East Los Angeles, Phillip has experienced the inequities within various underrepresented communities and has dedicated his life to helping those who need it most. Phillip was the first in his family to attend college, graduating from the University of California, Berkeley with a degree in Political Science. He is excited to continue his work at CODE2040 by helping Black and Latino/a students find success in the tech industry.

Fellows Recruitment Manager, Code2040 Code2040

Josilin Torrano
Josilin Torrano has been an in-house Recruiter and University Program Manager for eight years. Beginning her career as a University Relations intern while a student at UCLA. Since 2008, she has partnered with the best and brightest minds in the entertainment and tech industries to find exceptional talent. What she loves most about her job is connecting with people and helping them along in their careers. At Facebook, Joslin travels the world seeking top design, content strategy, and marketing talent. Having worked with professors, administrators and recrutiing partners, she understands the value of strong university relationships. Joslin has been featured in Vault, Career Contessa, Refinery29, Thought Café, Make It New: A History of Silicon Valley Design, and the Miracle Worker series.

University Product Recruiting Lead, Facebook, Career Contessa Mentor

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the conference about?

UR(x) is is a conference bringing together university job developers, university recruiting leaders, and student organization leaders in the tech industry.

Who should attend?

Unversity Job Developers (University Employer Relations, External Relations, Employer Development etc) University Recruiters, Student Organization Leaders, and organizations that help promote or facillitate connections between students and employers.

What the heck does "UR(x)" stand for?

UR = University Recruiting and x is a variable that we use to set the theme for the conference from year to year. This year x = Technical Recruiting.

I will be travelling to the conference, where should I stay?

There are a number of great hotel options in San Francisco and the surrounding area with easy access to both the Caltrain and BART.

How do I get to Lyft Headquarters?

Lyft HQ is conveniently located in the SoMa neighborhood in the city of San Francisco. Lyft is a short walk from the Caltrain Station at 4th & King Street and the BART. Parking is limited. so we adise against driving and suggest you take a Lyft 😉 .

Do you offer refunds?

We offer full refunds for your ticket before March 1st. We will not be able to provide refunds after the 1st of March. You are always welcome to transfer your ticket to someone else, simply let us know and we will process the request.

What meals are provided?

Breakfast, lunch, snacks, and all the coffee you can drink throughout the conference are included with your registration. Please let us know if if you have any special dietary requirements. We are happy to accommodate any dietary requests made in advance of the conference.

Who organizes UR(x)?

UR(x) is organized by a group of university administrators and university recruiters with support from great partners, volunteers, and sponsors.
